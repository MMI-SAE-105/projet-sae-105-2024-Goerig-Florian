The Hunger for Oil

The Hunger for Oil

A Prophetic Book Reinterpreted in 2500

In 2500, The Hunger for Oil is more than just a book: it is a prophetic text that anticipated the end of the fossil fuel era.

“Like it or not, oil has indeed become an indispensable part of our lives.” (The Hunger for Oil)

This book, published in the early 21st century, addressed the global dependence on oil with clarity and foresaw its disastrous consequences.

Today, in a world freed from this dependency, the text is revisited with fascination and gravity, as a warning whose implications still resonate.

Analysis of the Book

A Wake-Up Call for a Carefree Era

When Paul Roberts published The Hunger for Oil in 2004, he sought to warn the world about the imminent end of fossil fuel exploitation and the need to find sustainable alternatives.


The book accurately describes the economic and geopolitical cycles linked to oil, the struggles for its control, and the ecological consequences of its excessive use...

A Bygone World

A Civilization in Transition

In 2500, dependence on fossil fuels is but a distant memory, but the book The Hunger for Oil illustrates just how slow and painful this transition was.

The end of fossil fuels: an inevitable transition

Roberts highlights the international tensions, economic crises, and ecological disasters caused by the frantic race for oil exploitation.

For readers of our time, it is almost inconceivable that humanity continued to use fossil fuels for so long despite the warnings.

Reading this book provides a better understanding of the challenges faced by 21st-century societies, as well as their blindness to the depletion of natural resources.

“It is clear that we can no longer view the Earth as static. Viewing the Earth, its creatures, and its resources as frozen in time is to see merely a snapshot in a film that has been running for millions of years. The Earth must be seen as ever-changing, much like the palimpsests reused by monks with a deep sense of history.”
Excerpt from the book The Hunger for Oil

A Message for Future Generations

A Prophecy Fulfilled

Today, readers in 2500 see The Hunger for Oil as a prophecy fulfilled.

The author not only predicted the depletion of reserves but also the environmental and geopolitical disasters that would ensue.

Analyzing the solutions proposed in the book, such as the use of renewable energy and the need to change consumption models, people of our time can see that these long-ignored recommendations ultimately helped save humanity from a major energy crisis.

For the inhabitants of 2500, this book is seen as a call to action, though few governments of the time took its warnings seriously. Through this text, Roberts painted a realistic portrait of the challenges ahead, challenging the complacency of his era’s society.
