DeepWater Horizon

Deepwater Horizon

A Film from the Past, a Warning for the Future

In the year 2500, the film Deepwater Horizon is rediscovered as a major work from the end of the oil era.

Much more than a disaster film, it is now seen as a prophetic critique of the inherent risks of exploiting fossil fuels.

In an era where humanity has freed itself from this dependency, Deepwater Horizon echoes as a poignant reminder of the errors made by our ancestors in the 21st century.

Analysis of the Film

The Story of an Avoidable Catastrophe

Released in 2016, Deepwater Horizon depicts the tragic events of the explosion of the eponymous oil platform in 2010.

This film, praised at the time for its technical accuracy and human performances, vividly portrays the fragility of oil infrastructure.

The work illustrates the tension between the greed of major energy companies and the lives of workers caught in a disaster with disastrous environmental and human consequences.
