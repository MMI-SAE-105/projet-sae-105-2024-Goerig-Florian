Orelsan: The Smell of Gasoline

Orelsan and The Smell of Gasoline

When rap denounced the excesses of the modern world

By 2500, Orelsan's song The Smell of Gasoline, released in 2021, is seen as a prophetic and powerful work.

At the time, Orelsan used his art to denounce the social, political, and ecological flaws of a world in crisis.

While the society of 2500 has turned its back on the exploitation of fossil fuels and the divisions that once threatened humanity, this song and its video remain a powerful cry against the excesses of modern civilization.

A relentless critique of 2021 society

An unyielding and engaged text

The Smell of Gasoline begins with a sharp declaration: "The game is over, all our leaders have failed."

From the very first lines, Orelsan expresses the loss of trust in political leaders and the established order.

Through lyrics without a real chorus, he denounces a society ruled by fear, division, and collective anxiety.

For the inhabitants of 2500, this critique of the political and social systems of the early 21st century is perceived as a faithful portrait of an era when humanity seemed on the brink of collapse.

In his lyrics, Orelsan also criticizes the culture of clash and controversy that dominated the media and social networks, prioritizing sensation over reflection.

He highlights the flaws of a society where intelligence and dialogue are replaced by conflict and media manipulation, which, seen from 2500, seems almost incomprehensible, as these practices are now obsolete.

A video clip with powerful imagery

The denunciation of ecological crises

The video clip of The Smell of Gasoline amplifies the impact of Orelsan's message by incorporating shocking images denouncing ecological disasters and the excessive exploitation of natural resources.

Scenes of deforestation, polluted oceans, and landscapes ravaged by human activity are depicted.

By 2500, where society has embraced renewable energy and responsible management of resources, these images appear as relics of an irresponsible past.

For critics in 2500, the video serves to illustrate the urgency of Orelsan's message: the society of 2021 was heading for disaster if it continued down this destructive path.

These ecological visuals add a striking dimension to an already powerful text, emphasizing the gravity of the issues raised.
