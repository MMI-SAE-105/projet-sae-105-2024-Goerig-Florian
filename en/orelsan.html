Orelsan: The Smell of Gasoline

Orelsan and The Smell of Gasoline

When rap denounced the flaws of the modern world

In 2500, the song The Smell of Gasoline by OrelSan, released in 2021, is seen as a prophetic and powerful work.

At the time, Orelsan used his art to denounce the social, political, and environmental flaws of a world in crisis.

While the society of 2500 has turned away from the exploitation of fossil fuels and the divisions that once threatened humanity, this song and its music video are now remembered as an eloquent alarm against the excesses of modern civilization.

A relentless critique of 2021 society

A bold and uncompromising text

The Smell of Gasoline begins with a scathing statement: "The die is cast, all our leaders have failed."

From the very first lines, Orelsan expresses the loss of trust in political leaders and the established order.

Through a text without a true chorus, he denounces a society dominated by fear, division, and collective anxiety.

For the inhabitants of 2500, this critique of the political and social systems of the early 21st century is seen as a faithful depiction of a time when humanity seemed on the brink of collapse.

In the lyrics, Orelsan also criticizes the culture of clashes and controversy that dominated media and social networks, favoring sensation over reflection.

He points out the flaws of a society where intelligence and dialogue are replaced by conflict and media manipulation, which, from the perspective of 2500, seem almost incomprehensible, as these practices have become obsolete.

A music video with powerful imagery

Denouncing ecological crises

The music video for The Smell of Gasoline amplifies the message of Orelsan, integrating shocking imagery to denounce ecological disasters and the overexploitation of natural resources.

The video showcases scenes of deforestation, polluted oceans, and landscapes ravaged by human activity.

In 2500, where society has shifted to renewable energy and a responsible management of resources, these images are seen as relics of an irresponsible past.

For critics in 2500, the video underscores the urgency of Orelsan's message: the society of 2021 was heading for disaster if it continued on this destructive path.

These ecological images bring a striking visual dimension to an already powerful text, heightening the impact of the piece and the gravity of the issues it addresses.
