Orelsan: The Smell of Gasoline

Orelsan and "The Smell of Gasoline"

When rap denounced the flaws of the modern world

By 2500, Orelsan's song The Smell of Gasoline, released in 2021, is seen as a prophetic and powerful piece.

At the time, Orelsan used his art to denounce the social, political, and ecological flaws of a world in crisis.

While the society of 2500 has turned its back on fossil fuels and the divisions that once threatened humanity, this song and its video still resonate as a powerful cry against the excesses of modern civilization.

A relentless critique of 2021 society

An unrelenting and engaged text

The Smell of Gasoline begins with a sharp statement: "The game is over, all our leaders have failed."

From the first lyrics, Orelsan expresses the loss of trust in political leaders and the established order.

Through lyrics with no real refrain, he denounces a society dominated by fear, division, and collective anxiety.

He criticizes a society where intelligence and dialogue are replaced by conflict and media manipulation.
